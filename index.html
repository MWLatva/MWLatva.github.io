The Sunlab Cow

Fun Fact: The Sunlab cow's unofficial name is Daisy, this was decided before the CS department announced it to be named "Codee" (Drama?!)

Life before Lehigh:

The Sunlab cow was created by Tony Monroe in 1999 as a part of a command line tool "cowsay" and has become an icon in programming. Known for its friendly demeanor, the cow has been widely embraced in the computing. Even before Lehigh, the cow's simplicity and charm have made it a lasting symbol of command-line humor.

Life At Lehigh:

The Sunlab Cow Now welcomes Lehigh students to the Sunlab, a collection of computers running Linux for students to run homework projects on. The CSE department named the cow "Codee" in an Email sent by Professor Erle in fall 2023. The cow has become a CS legend, and even is featured on CSB merch.
